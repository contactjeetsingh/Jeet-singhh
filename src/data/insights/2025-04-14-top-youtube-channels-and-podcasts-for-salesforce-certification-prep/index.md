Preparing for Salesforce certifications can feel overwhelming at times, especially with the vast amount of content available online. Fortunately, several YouTube channels and podcasts have emerged as trusted resources for learners around the world. These platforms not only offer valuable insights into Salesforce concepts and exam strategies but also provide motivation and guidance from certified professionals who have walked the same path. In this blog, we explore some of the best YouTube channels and podcasts that can help you prepare effectively for your Salesforce certification journey.

## Why YouTube and Podcasts Are Game-Changers for Salesforce Prep

While traditional study materials like Trailhead and official guides are essential, YouTube and podcasts bring a more conversational and practical angle to learning. These platforms often break down complex topics into digestible formats, share real-world use cases, and offer free tutorials, mock exams, and tips from experienced professionals. For those balancing work or studies, podcasts are particularly useful because you can learn on the go—whether you’re commuting, exercising, or doing chores.

## YouTube Channels Every Salesforce Aspirant Should Follow

One of the most popular destinations for Salesforce certification prep is YouTube. Channels like **Salesforce Hulk**, run by Salesforce MVP Shrey Sharma, offer highly visual and easy-to-understand tutorials for Admin, Developer, and App Builder certifications. His content is especially helpful for beginners, thanks to its clear explanations and structured playlists.

Another great channel is **Focus on Force**, known for its detailed breakdowns of certification topics and exam strategies. Although they offer paid content on their website, their YouTube channel gives free sneak peeks into the kind of structured guidance they provide.

**Wizlabs** and **Talent Stacker** are also valuable resources. Wizlabs covers Salesforce concepts along with practice exam tips, while Talent Stacker shares success stories and behind-the-scenes journeys of those who’ve landed Salesforce jobs after certification.

For technical deep dives, **SFDCFacts Academy** by Deepika Khana is a must-watch. This channel dives into the developer side of Salesforce, with hands-on demos, LWC tutorials, and preparation content for Platform Developer I and II exams.

## Must-Listen Podcasts for Salesforce Learners

Podcasts offer a more flexible and conversational way to consume Salesforce knowledge. One standout podcast is the **Salesforce Admins Podcast**, where each episode covers a specific topic related to admin roles, certifications, and career paths. Hosted by Salesforce insiders, this podcast helps you stay current with platform updates and real-world best practices.

Another noteworthy show is the **Good Day, Sir! Podcast**, hosted by two seasoned Salesforce developers. This podcast focuses more on developer content, ideal for those preparing for advanced certifications or trying to deepen their technical knowledge.

The **Talent Stacker Career Development Podcast** is great for those looking to break into the Salesforce ecosystem. It covers certification preparation, job search strategies, and interviews with individuals who’ve successfully transitioned into Salesforce careers—making it both informative and motivational.

If you're interested in hearing directly from certified experts, check out the **WizardCast** podcast. It brings in guest architects, consultants, and instructors to discuss exam tips, certification updates, and industry trends.

## How to Make the Most of These Resources

While these YouTube channels and podcasts are excellent tools, they work best when combined with consistent hands-on practice. Try to pair what you learn in a video or podcast with actual Salesforce org experimentation. For instance, if a podcast explains record types or security models, log in to your Trailhead Playground and try building a simple setup. This not only reinforces your understanding but also boosts your confidence during the exam.

Set a schedule to consume content regularly—perhaps one video during lunch breaks or one podcast episode during your evening walk. Over time, this passive and active combination of learning will make a significant impact on your retention and confidence.

## Final Thoughts

Salesforce certification prep doesn't have to be a solo or boring experience. With the rise of content creators, MVPs, and passionate community leaders, you now have access to a wealth of free and engaging resources. YouTube channels and podcasts bring expert advice, industry trends, and actionable tips straight to your fingertips. Whether you're a complete beginner or brushing up for advanced certifications, tuning into these platforms could be the edge you need to pass with confidence and move forward in your Salesforce career.
